################ SEARCH SOURCES ################

- A Toy Model of Universality: Reverse Engineering How Networks Learn Group Operations (Chughtai, Chan & Nanda, ICML 2023)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.03025  [high]
  THE key distinguisher for Thread 4. Establishes cross-seed universality only for GROUP-THEORETIC representations, and even there finds only WEAK universality: across 50 seeds (S5, MLP) models learn DIFFERENT irreps, in different numbers and different orders, contradicting strong universality. Crucially it does NOT study world-model/state-tracking reps, so the specific claim 'cross-seed universality of a world-model representation' is NOT covered here — this is the paper to cite as the prior art the proposed work goes beyond.

- Revisiting the Othello World Model Hypothesis (2025)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2503.04421v1  [high]
  Highest scoop risk for the universality angle. Claims language models 'regardless of architecture or scale learn similar patterns from Othello game sequences' and 'converge on universal representations when trained on structured data,' using CKA and cosine similarity across models. This is the closest existing demonstration of cross-model universality of a WORLD-MODEL rep (not group reps) — must be read carefully to see whether it covers cross-seed universality and, critically, whether it ties universality/emergence-shape to latent-state structure (it appears not to make the discrete-vs-continuous emergence-sharpness argument).

- Linear Latent World Models in Simple Transformers: A Case Study on Othello-GPT (2023)
  https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.07582  [high]
  Reports that 'all Othello-GPT models tested present a similar ability to encode the world representation for a given layer' — first-layer probe accuracy and losses are similar across GPT models, and mapping is most accurate in deep layers. Direct evidence of cross-run consistency of the linear world-model rep via ground-truth linear probes, i.e. partial coverage of the cross-seed universality claim, but without a CKA seed-ensemble study or any structure-determines-emergence-shape framing.

- Actually, Othello-GPT Has A Linear Emergent World Representation — Neel Nanda
  https://www.neelnanda.io/mechanistic-interpretability/othello  [high]
  The canonical linear-probe follow-up establishing that the Othello board state is linearly recoverable (mine/theirs relative to current player). Foundational for the ground-truth-linear-probe methodology the proposed paper would use, but treats a single model and does not address cross-seed universality, developmental timing, or emergence sharpness as a function of latent-state structure.

- Towards Universality: Studying Mechanistic Similarity Across Language Model Architectures (2024)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2410.06672v1  [medium]
  Extends the universality question to mechanistic similarity across different LM architectures. Relevant as adjacent 2024 prior art on how far universality of learned mechanisms holds, but is about architecture-invariance of mechanisms in real LMs rather than cross-seed universality of a controlled, ground-truth world-model/state-tracking representation — leaves the toy-setting seed-ensemble world-model universality gap open.

- TRACE: Tracking the Emergence of Semantic Representations in Transformers (2025)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17998  [medium]
  Developmental-dynamics companion evidence: a diagnostic framework detecting phase transitions in representation formation, showing curvature collapse and dimension stabilization coincide with emerging syntactic/semantic accuracy. Relevant to the 'when does the representation form over training' half of the claim, but tracks emergence timing generally and does NOT relate emergence sharpness to discrete-vs-continuous latent-state structure or measure universality across seeds — so it supports the developmental method without scooping the structure-to-emergence-shape thesis.

- The Emergence of Sparse Attention: Impact of Data Distribution and Benefits of Repetition (Zucchet et al., 2025)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17863  [high]
  CLOSEST 2024-26 prior art on the primary angle. In a controlled toy transformer setting, the authors show emergence TIMING follows power laws set by task structure, architecture, and optimizer, and derive a theoretical framework for how the data/task structure governs the shape and timing of a capability's emergence. This is the paper most likely to be seen as partially scooping a 'structure determines emergence-shape' claim; the gap is that it studies attention-pattern emergence, not ground-truth linear probes of a known discrete-vs-continuous latent state across a seed ensemble.

- The Quantization Model of Neural Scaling / 'quanta' (Michaud et al., 2023, NeurIPS)
  https://arxiv.org/pdf/2303.13506  [high]
  Direct backbone of the 'discrete latent -> sharp emergence' half of the claim: capabilities are 'quantized' into discrete quanta learned in frequency order, so each individual skill appears as a sharp phase transition while the aggregate loss is smooth. Establishes discreteness -> abruptness, but does NOT contrast it against a continuous/additive latent state (e.g. spatial position) producing gradual emergence, nor use ground-truth linear probes. Precisely defines what remains unfilled.

- LLM Emergence: Are Capabilities Real or Metric Artifacts? (summary of Schaeffer, Miranda & Koyejo 2023, 'Are Emergent Abilities a Mirage?')
  https://mbrenndoerfer.com/writing/llm-emergence-metrics-measurement-artifacts  [high]
  Covers the core counter-argument any reviewer will raise: apparent sharp emergence is often an artifact of discontinuous/thresholded metrics (accuracy, exact-match); replotting with continuous metrics (cross-entropy) yields smooth curves. A 'structure determines sharpness' paper must control for this by using continuous ground-truth linear-probe accuracy of the latent state rather than task metrics — which is exactly the design proposed. Confirms the claim is not already established and shows the methodological bar.

- A Review of Developmental Interpretability in Large Language Models (2025)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.15841  [medium]
  Maps thread 2 (developmental interpretability / SLT / grokking / induction-head phase changes). Useful for situating the claim within work that studies WHEN structure forms during training. Reviews phase transitions and progress measures but, per the survey framing, does not consolidate a discrete-vs-continuous-latent -> sharp-vs-gradual law, confirming the framing is open rather than established.

- When Probing Accuracy Saturates, Fragility Resolves: A Complementary Metric for LLM Pre-Training Analysis (2026)
  https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.11375  [medium]
  Recent, adversarially relevant: proposes complementary linear-probe-based metrics for tracking representation development over pre-training, noting probing accuracy can saturate while finer structure keeps changing. Directly bears on the proposed methodology (ground-truth linear probes across training) and warns that raw probe accuracy alone may under-resolve the emergence shape — a design consideration reviewers would demand be addressed.

- Call for Papers — Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop (ICML 2026 / NeurIPS 2025 series)
  https://mechinterpworkshop.com/cfp/  [high]
  The single most realistic venue for this claim, and the page that reveals the review bar. Non-archival (so it can precede a full-conference version). Explicitly welcomes 'conceptual and foundational work on frameworks and definitions' and 'understanding model internals and representations' — a 'task structure determines emergence-shape' thesis lands squarely here. Crucially, the stated acceptance criterion is that strong empirical work must 'articulate specific falsifiable hypotheses' or show 'clear practical benefits over well-implemented baselines,' with double-blind review and required open-sourced code/data. This tells you exactly what to foreground: a crisp falsifiable hypothesis (discrete latent -> sharp, continuous/additive -> gradual), a controlled toy setup with ground-truth linear probes, and a seed ensemble as the baseline-beating rigor. Page limits: 4pg short / 8pg (ICML) or 5/9 (NeurIPS), excluding refs/appendix — a workshop-scale contribution, not a full paper.

- Convergent World Representations and Divergent Tasks (Feb 2026)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00533  [high]
  SINGLE CLOSEST PRIOR ART to the whole proposal, and the most likely partial scoop. Trains transformers on a CONTINUOUS/SPATIAL latent world (5,075 real city coordinates) across 7 geometric tasks. Directly does two of the paper's three pillars: (1) developmental dynamics — 'representations are essentially fixed in the first ~15% of training,' with an explicit loss-plateau phase (random reps -> cluster nearby cities -> world-aligned geometry as loss drops), and linear-probe R2 rising before angle accuracy ('hidden progress'); (2) cross-seed CKA — 'substantial variability even across seeds for the same task,' with multi-task training raising CKA to ~0.85. CRITICAL GAP IT LEAVES OPEN: it studies ONLY the continuous/spatial case and never contrasts it against a DISCRETE/algorithmic latent to test whether structure determines emergence SHARPNESS. Confirms the continuous-side prediction (gradual/plateau emergence) but does not make the discrete-vs-continuous claim. Adversarial read: a reviewer will ask why the proposed work isn't just adding the discrete arm to this paper.

- Emergent World Models and Latent Variable Estimation in Chess-Playing Language Models (Karvonen 2024)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.15498  [high]
  Chess-GPT: linear probes recover board state (discrete algorithmic latent) at ~99.2% square accuracy from next-token PGN training, plus estimation of a genuinely LATENT variable (player Elo), with causal skill/board interventions. Strongest evidence that a discrete algorithmic latent is linearly represented, and Karvonen's companion blog notes the world model strengthens with model/data scale — but the paper does NOT chart probe accuracy over training checkpoints to characterize emergence as sharp vs gradual, and does NOT run a seed ensemble or CKA. Useful as the discrete-latent testbed; leaves the developmental-shape and universality questions unanswered.

- Emergent World Representations: Exploring a Sequence Model Trained on a Synthetic Task (Li et al. 2022/2023)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.13382  [high]
  The origin of world-model probing on a controlled synthetic task with a known ground-truth latent (Othello board), and the closest thing to a developmental study on the discrete side: it probes across TEMPORAL CHECKPOINTS (every ~5,000 steps), reporting that prediction loss and probe direction accuracy co-improve during training — evidence the internal world model tracks training progress. But it frames this as coordinated learning, NOT as a sharp phase transition, does not contrast against a continuous/additive latent, and runs a single model rather than a seed ensemble. The 'co-improvement across checkpoints' observation is the empirical hook the proposal must differentiate from.

- Exposing Attention Glitches with Flip-Flop Language Modeling (Liu et al. 2023)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.00946  [medium]
  Directly supports the DISCRETE-latent-as-toy-testbed framing and hints at the sharpness claim. Flip-flop LM is a minimal discrete STATE-TRACKING task (write/read/ignore over a stored bit) with a clean algorithmic latent. Shows Transformers learn it in a brittle, non-smooth way ('attention glitches' — intermittent long-tail failures) rather than converging gracefully, which is suggestive of sharp/unstable emergence for discrete state. Does not use ground-truth linear probes of the latent over training, no continuous-latent comparison, no cross-seed CKA — but it is a ready-made discrete-latent task and a citation for 'discrete state tracking behaves qualitatively differently.'

- (How) Do Language Models Track State? (2025)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.02854  [medium]
  Recent state-tracking mechanistic study (permutation-composition, a discrete/algebraic latent) showing models learn one of two identifiable mechanisms and — key for the proposal — that 'the emergence of these mechanisms can be predicted and controlled,' i.e. emergence timing is a studied variable. This is the closest work explicitly treating WHEN a discrete state-tracking capability emerges, and different seeds landing on different mechanisms speaks to (non-)universality. Gap: it centers on mechanism identity and robustness, not on linear-probe emergence SHAPE (sharp vs gradual) as a function of latent structure, and does not pair it against a continuous latent — leaving the specific 'structure -> emergence-shape' contrast unclaimed.

- Nanda, Chan, Lieberum, Smith, Steinhardt (2023) — Progress Measures for Grokking via Mechanistic Interpretability (ICLR 2023)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.05217  [high]
  The anchor paper for Thread 2. Reverse-engineers modular-addition transformers (a DISCRETE/ALGORITHMIC task with a Fourier/rotation solution) and shows grokking is a phase-change decomposable into memorization → circuit formation → cleanup via continuous 'progress measures.' Directly relevant because it studies a discrete-latent algorithmic task and finds sharp qualitative emergence underlain by continuous internal restructuring — but it does NOT contrast this against a continuous/additive-latent task, so the discrete-vs-continuous emergence-shape claim is left open. Closest methodological template (progress measures / probing internal structure over training) for the proposed paper.

- Hoogland, Wang, Farrugia-Roberts, Carroll, Wei, Murfet (2024) — The Developmental Landscape of In-Context Learning
  https://www.suswei.com/publication/hoogland-2024-icl1/hoogland-2024-icl1.pdf  [high]
  Core Timaeus/SLT developmental-interpretability result: in-context learning emerges in DISCRETE, interpretable developmental stages, detectable model- and data-agnostically by probing loss-landscape geometry (Local Learning Coefficient + weight/behavior distance). Establishes that devinterp CAN detect stagewise (sharp) structure formation over training, but frames it via loss-landscape degeneracy, NOT via a discrete-vs-continuous distinction in the task's latent state — the exact gap the proposed paper targets. Key prior art to distinguish against.

- Chen, Hoogland, van Wingerden, Lehalleur, Murfet et al. (2024) — Loss Landscape Degeneracy and Stagewise Development in Transformers
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02364  [high]
  The SLT/LLC foundation for developmental interpretability: shows transformer training decomposes into discrete developmental stages quantified by the Local Learning Coefficient (degeneracy of the loss landscape). Shows the community ties emergence timing/sharpness to loss-geometry degeneracy rather than to the discrete/continuous STRUCTURE of the task's ground-truth latent state. Confirms SLT/devinterp has NOT made the specific 'task-structure determines emergence-shape' claim, strengthening a novelty argument while being the most likely reviewer-cited neighbor.

- Olsson et al., Anthropic (2022) — In-Context Learning and Induction Heads
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.11895  [high]
  Canonical 'phase change' during training: induction heads form abruptly, coinciding with a sharp jump in in-context learning, with causal architectural interventions shifting the transition's onset. Provides the sharp-emergence exemplar for a mechanism tracking a discrete/copy (algorithmic) structure, and demonstrates cross-run reliability of the phenomenon. Relevant as evidence that sharp phase changes attach to discrete/algorithmic circuits, but it never contrasts this with a graded emergence for continuous latent structure, nor uses ground-truth linear probes of a known latent — the proposed contribution's distinctive move.

- Michaud, Liu, Girit, Tegmark (2023) — The Quantization Model of Neural Scaling (NeurIPS 2023)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.13506  [high]
  Most direct mechanistic account of WHY some abilities emerge sharply: knowledge/skills are 'quantized' into discrete indivisible computational modules (quanta); smooth scaling laws average over discrete jumps as quanta are learned in frequency order. This is the strongest existing statement that DISCRETENESS of the underlying computation produces abrupt/sharp emergence — a partial scoop of one half of the claim. Crucially, it does not treat CONTINUOUS/additive latent structure as producing gradual emergence, and works at the scaling-law/skill level rather than via controlled linear probes of a known latent — leaving the paired discrete-vs-continuous, probe-based contrast unfilled.

- On the Emergence and Test-Time Use of Structural Information in Large Language Models (2026)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2601.17869  [medium]
  Adversarial novelty flag / closest potential scoop on the sharp-vs-gradual framing: reports that single-token / structurally-simple tasks show sharp, step-like sigmoidal emergence while compositional/multi-token tasks rise gradually with no sharp inflection — i.e. it explicitly ties emergence SHAPE to task structure. This is the nearest recent work to the proposed thesis and must be read carefully to delineate the gap: it operates at the behavioral/task-complexity level in LLMs (compositional vs simple), NOT the specific discrete-algorithmic vs continuous-additive LATENT-STATE distinction measured with ground-truth linear probes across a seed ensemble in a controlled toy setting. Confirm exact claims before relying on the summary.

- Quiet Feature Learning in Algorithmic Tasks (Naidu, Wang, Bergen, Paturi, 2025)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.03997  [high]
  THE SINGLE CLOSEST SCOOP CANDIDATE for this angle. Controlled synthetic/algorithmic tasks; uses LINEAR PROBES of ground-truth latent state ACROSS RANDOM SEEDS to detect when structured representations form. Explicitly reports that discrete algorithmic latents emerge SHARPLY/abruptly (representations form 'quietly' on a loss plateau, then a sudden loss drop), and the fetched text contrasts this with more GRADUAL emergence for continuous latent structure — i.e. the same discrete=sharp / continuous=gradual axis the user wants to claim. GAP IT LEAVES: its headline contribution is the decoupling of feature-learning from behavioral mastery ('quiet' features) on discrete algorithmic tasks; it does NOT run a controlled discrete-vs-continuous (e.g. spatial/additive-position) comparison as the primary manipulated variable, nor characterize emergence SHAPE as a function of latent geometry with a seed ensemble. That residual comparison is the defensible remaining contribution — but this paper is close enough that any submission must cite and explicitly differentiate from it.

- Emergence and Localisation of Semantic Role Circuits in LLMs (Aljaafari, Carvalho, Freitas, 2025)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2511.20910  [medium]
  Adversarially important: a NEAR-MISS that partially CONTRADICTS the claim. Studies compositional/positional (semantic-role) features — exactly the 'requires integrating positional relationships' side — and finds structural metrics 'evolve smoothly throughout training, indicating continuous refinement rather than discrete transitions.' Consistent with the user's continuous-latent-=>-gradual half, but it uses circuit-attribution (EAP-IG), NOT ground-truth linear probes, and does not run a discrete-vs-continuous controlled contrast or seed ensemble. Shows the field is actively linking feature TYPE to emergence SHAPE but has not isolated the discrete-vs-continuous latent-geometry variable with clean probes — the gap the user can fill.

- TRACE: Tracking the Emergence of Semantic Representations in Transformers (Aljaafari, Carvalho, Freitas, 2025)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2505.17998  [medium]
  The closest METHODOLOGICAL template: linear probes trained on DETERMINISTIC GROUND-TRUTH labels from a synthetic dataset (ABSynth25K), repeated across at least 5 SEEDS, tracking when representations emerge. Finds a coordinated SHARP phase transition (curvature flattening + intrinsic-dimension shift + probe jump) marking memorization->generalization. But it uses a SINGLE task structure and does NOT vary discrete-vs-continuous latent geometry, so it cannot speak to 'structure determines emergence-shape.' Demonstrates the exact experimental apparatus (ground-truth linear probes + seed ensemble + developmental tracking) is already accepted practice — which both lowers the novelty of the METHOD and pinpoints that the novel claim must live in the controlled structural CONTRAST.

- Transformers Linearly Represent Highly Structured World Models (Kniazev & Fijalkow, 2026)
  https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.18847  [medium]
  Closest on the continuous/spatial + linear-world-model side (the 'continuous/additive latent state e.g. spatial position' half of the claim). Establishes that transformers encode structured world-model state LINEARLY, extending Othello-GPT-style world-model probing to richer structured latents. However it does NOT study DEVELOPMENTAL dynamics (when the representation forms, sharp vs gradual) and does not contrast continuous-vs-discrete emergence shape or run a seed ensemble on timing. The adjacent, unfilled seam: nobody in this world-model-probing lineage has tied WHEN/HOW-SHARPLY the linear world-model forms to the discrete-vs-continuous structure of the underlying latent.

- A Ghost Mechanism: An Analytical Model of Abrupt Learning (2025)
  https://arxiv.org/html/2501.02378v1  [medium]
  Provides a mechanistic THEORY for WHY abrupt (sharp) learning occurs: in a latent-variable toy model, a 'ghost' fixed point creates very slow latent-variable dynamics, producing a plateau followed by an abrupt loss drop and gradient-norm spike. Directly relevant to the 'discrete/algorithmic => sharp phase transition' half and to the developmental-dynamics framing, and could supply the mechanistic explanation the user's empirical claim would need. But it is a dynamical-systems account of abruptness in general, NOT a discrete-vs-continuous structural comparison with ground-truth probes across seeds — so it complements rather than scoops the claim.

- (central) Cross-seed universality has been demonstrated for group-theoretic / algorithmic tasks: networks trained to compose finite group operations consistently converge on the same representation-theory-based algorithm across random seeds, and the full family of features/circuits can be characterized. This establishes universality for GROUP-THEORETIC representations specifically (the exact contrast the research question draws against world-model/state-tracking representations).

- (supporting) Universality holds at the level of the abstract algorithm, NOT at the level of exact circuits or their training-time developmental order: which specific circuits a given network learns, and the ORDER in which they develop, are arbitrary across runs. This qualifies any claim that cross-seed universality extends to the developmental dynamics / emergence timing of a representation.

- (supporting) The universality result is confined to a DISCRETE, algorithmic latent structure (finite-group composition via representation theory) and is verified by reverse-engineering weights/logits plus ablations, not by ground-truth linear probes of a known latent state, and not compared against any continuous/additive latent task. This leaves the discrete-vs-continuous emergence-shape comparison, and probe-based cross-seed universality of a world-model, unaddressed.

- (central) The paper does not study training-time emergence dynamics, phase transitions, or discrete-vs-continuous latent structure; it evaluates only final/converged representations and reports capability change solely as a function of dataset SIZE, not training checkpoints, so the 'when/how the world model forms over training' question remains unaddressed here.

- (supporting) The paper deliberately abandons the ground-truth linear-probe methodology used by prior Othello-GPT work (Li et al., Nanda) in favor of representation alignment, explicitly criticizing probing classifiers — so it does NOT use ground-truth linear probes of the known latent state, unlike the proposed developmental-probing approach.

- (supporting) The paper measures representational similarity of board features ACROSS different model ARCHITECTURES (not across random seeds), using cosine similarity of aligned features rather than CKA, reporting e.g. 93.1% similarity between GPT-2 (decoder-only) and Bart (encoder-decoder).
